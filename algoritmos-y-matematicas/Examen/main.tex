\[
    S =\frac{1}{1} \binom{n}{0} + \frac{1}{2} \binom{n}{1} + \frac{1}{3} \binom{n}{2} + ... +\frac{1}{n+1} \binom{n}{n}
\]
\newline
Podemos definir \[ \binom{n}{k} = \frac{n!}{k!(n-k)!}\]
